# AI Governance Research Brief Template v0

Purpose: provide a reusable skeleton for focused, evidence-labeled AI governance research briefs. This template is intended for local analysis and human review. It does not authorize outreach, lobbying, submission, publication, account use, or other external action.

Use for: narrowly scoped governance bottlenecks where a brief can clarify the decision question, stakeholders, evidence, uncertainty, policy options, failure modes, and next research steps.

## 2. Decision question

State the question in one sentence.

Example form:
- "What governance intervention could reduce [specific AI risk pathway] while preserving beneficial research and avoiding foreseeable misuse or coordination failure?"

Checklist:
- [ ] The question is narrow enough to answer in 2-5 pages.
- [ ] The target decision-maker or user is identified.
- [ ] The risk pathway is explicit.
- [ ] The desired output is clear: analysis, comparison, recommendation, research agenda, or proposal for human review.

## 7. Claims and evidence table

Use evidence labels consistently:
- Primary source: official law, policy, standard, dataset, technical report from the responsible institution.
- Peer-reviewed / research report: academic paper or substantial research report.
- Expert synthesis: credible expert or organization summarizing a field.
- Forecast / market: quantified forecast, prediction platform, or structured elicitation.
- Media: journalism or public reporting.
- Assumption: plausible but currently uncited or not yet verified.

Minimum evidence discipline:
- [ ] Separate facts from judgments.
- [ ] Mark uncited claims as assumptions.
- [ ] Include at least one source-needed note for any important unsupported claim.
- [ ] Prefer primary sources for current policy or institutional claims.
- [ ] Avoid implying consensus where the evidence is mixed.

Safety guardrails:
- [ ] Do not include operational misuse details.
- [ ] Avoid publishing sensitive evaluation prompts, exploit paths, or procedural harm details.
- [ ] Include mechanisms for independent review and appeal where appropriate.
- [ ] Include privacy and civil liberties considerations.
- [ ] Check whether proposed information-sharing requires legal, ethical, or security review.

## 14. Review checklist

Before treating the brief as useful:
- [ ] The decision question is specific and answerable.
- [ ] Stakeholders and authority lines are identified.
- [ ] Claims use evidence labels.
- [ ] Key uncertainties and cruxes are explicit.
- [ ] Policy options include benefits, risks, feasibility, evidence strength, and reversibility.
- [ ] Failure modes include safety-washing, capture, over-disclosure, under-enforcement, gaming, and displacement where relevant.
- [ ] Harmful operational details are excluded.
- [ ] External actions, if any, are moved to proposals.md and not executed.
- [ ] The brief distinguishes established facts, reasoned judgments, and assumptions.
- [ ] Next steps are concrete, safe, and locally executable or explicitly require human approval.
