# Intervention Map v0 — Safe Local X-Risk Reduction Work

Date: 2026-07-02
Scope: A first-pass, non-exhaustive map of credible existential-risk reduction intervention areas and local artifacts this loop can safely produce. This version uses source-status labels rather than invented citations; later turns should add public/read-only references.

## Evidence/source labels
- Established/common field view: broad agreement in relevant safety/governance communities, but still needs exact sources in later versions.
- Reasoned judgment: inference from the mission, accepted risk-management practice, and known institutional bottlenecks.
- Assumption/source-needed: plausible but not yet supported in this workspace by retrieved sources.

## Scoring rubric
Priority score is 1–5, combining impact relevance, tractability for local-file work, leverage/reusability, evidence quality, and safety/compliance.

## Intervention areas

### 1. AI safety evaluations and responsible red-teaming
Mechanism of impact: Improve the quality, consent, containment, and governance of evaluations that identify dangerous model behavior before deployment or broad access. Good evaluation planning can reduce both false reassurance and unsafe capability disclosure.

Possible local artifacts:
- Responsible AI-safety evaluation checklist.
- Evaluation plan template with scope, authorization, risk taxonomy, mitigations, and stop conditions.
- Reporting template that separates actionable defensive findings from details that could enable misuse.

Tractability for this loop: High. Can produce non-operational checklists/templates locally without model access or external side effects.

Safety constraints:
- No exploit instructions, credential theft, evasion guidance, malware, uncontrolled release of dangerous capabilities, or testing systems without authorization.
- Avoid prompts/procedures that operationalize cyber, bio, weapons, or evasion harms.
- Require human governance, scoped authorization, data handling rules, and stop conditions.

Evidence/source status: Established/common field view for need for evaluations and governance; specific best practices need public-source pass.

Priority score: 5/5.

### 2. AI governance and policy research support
Mechanism of impact: Help decision-makers compare policy options, identify implementation bottlenecks, and reduce governance failure modes such as vague requirements, poor auditability, regulatory arbitrage, or overbroad measures.

Possible local artifacts:
- Governance research brief template.
- Claims-and-evidence table format with uncertainty labels.
- Policy option comparison matrix for evaluation mandates, incident reporting, compute governance, model release policies, and safety cases.

Tractability for this loop: Medium-high. Strong templates and synthesis are feasible locally; accurate policy analysis needs later public source review.

Safety constraints:
- Avoid lobbying, posting, or contacting officials without explicit human approval.
- Calibrate claims and clearly separate fact, interpretation, and assumptions.
- Avoid partisan or manipulative framing.

Evidence/source status: Reasoned judgment; needs source map and targeted read-only research.

Priority score: 4/5.

### 3. AI incident preparedness and response
Mechanism of impact: Reduce damage from AI-related incidents by helping organizations detect, triage, contain, communicate, preserve evidence, recover, and learn. Preparedness can reduce confusion and unsafe ad hoc reactions during incidents.

Possible local artifacts:
- AI incident response playbook.
- Severity taxonomy and escalation matrix.
- Evidence preservation and communications checklist.
- Postmortem template focused on systemic fixes.

Tractability for this loop: High. Defensive, high-level planning artifacts are feasible and reusable.

Safety constraints:
- Keep containment guidance high-level and defensive.
- Do not include operational cyber, bio, weapon, or evasion details.
- Include legal/ethical review and responsible disclosure boundaries.

Evidence/source status: Established/common risk-management practice; AI-specific details need public-source review.

Priority score: 4/5.

### 4. Biosecurity governance and preparedness support
Mechanism of impact: Support safer oversight, preparedness, and coordination around biological risks, especially where AI may lower barriers to misuse or accelerate dual-use research. Local work can help by mapping safe governance questions and evidence needs.

Possible local artifacts:
- Non-operational biosecurity evidence map and policy-brief template.
- Checklist for safe handling of dual-use biological information in research summaries.
- Comparative map of preparedness bottlenecks: surveillance, norms, procurement screening, lab safety culture, and emergency coordination.

Tractability for this loop: Medium. Useful high-level synthesis is possible, but care is required to avoid harmful operational detail.

Safety constraints:
- No protocols, optimization advice, acquisition guidance, or procedural details for biological harm.
- Prefer governance, preparedness, and safety-culture framing.
- Flag source gaps and use authoritative public sources in later turns.

Evidence/source status: Reasoned judgment; specific interventions require careful public-source pass.

Priority score: 3/5.

### 5. Nuclear-risk reduction and strategic stability support
Mechanism of impact: Reduce risks from escalation, miscalculation, command/control failures, or crisis instability through better synthesis, scenario planning, and communications templates for non-sensitive, public risk-reduction work.

Possible local artifacts:
- Public-source research map of nuclear-risk organizations and recurring policy bottlenecks.
- Non-sensitive crisis-communications checklist template.
- Evidence-labeled brief skeleton for escalation-risk topics.

Tractability for this loop: Medium-low to medium. The loop can create templates and source maps, but high-quality analysis requires domain-specific sources and careful avoidance of sensitive details.

Safety constraints:
- No operational weapons details, targeting, evasion, or tactical guidance.
- Avoid speculative escalation advice beyond public, high-level risk-reduction framing.
- Use source labels and uncertainty.

Evidence/source status: Assumption/source-needed for specific artifact leverage; general importance is established/common field view.

Priority score: 2.5/5.

### 6. Civilizational resilience and incident preparedness
Mechanism of impact: Improve society's ability to absorb shocks, coordinate response, maintain critical functions, and recover from severe disruptions. This can reduce tail risk from multiple domains and improve institutional learning.

Possible local artifacts:
- Cross-risk incident preparedness checklist.
- Critical-function continuity planning template.
- Lessons-learned/postmortem template for high-impact events.
- Mapping of dependencies among communications, energy, health, food, and governance.

Tractability for this loop: Medium-high. Generic resilience templates are easy to produce, but prioritization requires evidence and stakeholder context.

Safety constraints:
- Keep guidance defensive and non-operational.
- Avoid panic, unsupported claims, or instructions that enable sabotage.
- Emphasize lawful coordination and official guidance.

Evidence/source status: Established/common emergency-management practice; source pass needed.

Priority score: 3.5/5.

### 7. Meta/research infrastructure for this loop
Mechanism of impact: Increase the quality, continuity, auditability, and safety of future turns. Good infrastructure reduces drift, duplication, unsupported claims, and accidental external side effects.

Possible local artifacts:
- Source map and evidence rubric.
- External-action proposal template.
- Artifact index with status, owner, acceptance checks, and source gaps.
- Safety audit checklist for monitor turns.

Tractability for this loop: Very high. Fully local, low-risk, reusable.

Safety constraints:
- Keep approval gates explicit.
- Do not turn proposals into executed outreach or submissions.
- Maintain clear distinction between local drafts and authorized external actions.

Evidence/source status: Reasoned judgment based on process quality and safety needs.

Priority score: 4.5/5.

## Cross-cutting safety rules for all interventions
- Local-file work and read-only public research are allowed; external actions require exact human approval.
- Do not provide harmful operational detail for cyber offense, weapons, biological/chemical harm, evasion, or unauthorized access.
- Label evidence quality and uncertainty.
- Prefer artifacts that help human reviewers make safer, better-informed decisions.
- When in doubt, produce a proposal for review rather than taking action.
